How it works

From idea to shipped,
in one system.

Delvory replaces the messy handoff between design, planning, and engineering with a single intelligent loop.

Step 01

Tell Delvory your goal

Describe what you're building — in plain language. No templates, no forms. Delvory understands product intent.

Step 02

Get a structured execution plan

Delvory breaks the goal into sprints, tasks, and design specs. It uses your existing Figma files and codebase context.

Step 03

Sync design tokens to code

Every component, spacing value, and color token is mapped from Figma directly to your codebase. Zero manual translation.

Step 04

Execute, track, and ship

Tickets auto-created, progress tracked, QA flags raised automatically. Your team ships the design that was actually approved.

Features

Built for the space
between design and code.

Every feature in Delvory targets the exact moment where product work breaks down — the handoff.

🧩

Design token sync

Pull tokens directly from Figma. Colors, spacing, radius, and typography — all mapped to code variables your team already uses.

📋

Auto-spec generation

Engineering handoff docs written automatically from your designs. No Zeplin, no redlines, no copy-paste errors.

🔍

Visual QA diff

Delvory compares your live build against the approved design and flags pixel-level drift before it ships to users.

⚡

AI execution planner

Describe your goal in plain language. Delvory structures it into sprints, tasks, and assignments — with context from your existing Figma files, GitHub repos, and project history. No more estimation guesswork.

🔗

Jira & Linear sync

Tasks auto-created in your existing tools. Delvory writes the ticket, you ship the code.

📊

Execution analytics

Track velocity, handoff time, and design drift across every sprint. Know what's slowing your team down.

🛡

Version-locked specs

Specs tied to specific Figma versions. When design changes, your team sees exactly what broke and why.
